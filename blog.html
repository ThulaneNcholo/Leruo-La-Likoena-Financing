Is Your Bookkeeper Doing These Things?

Running a business is hard enough without having to worry if your books are in order or if SARS is going to surprise you. The right bookkeeper isn’t just ticking boxes — they’re actively helping you grow.

Giving You Clear, Easy-to-Understand Reports

You should know exactly how much you earned, spent, and owe every month. Your accountant should provide:

Income vs Expenses breakdown

Profit/Loss statement

Simple commentary you can understand

If your monthly reports are confusing or inconsistent, it’s time to talk.

Reminding You of SARS Deadlines

VAT returns, PAYE, provisional tax… it’s a lot. A good accountant:

Sends monthly SARS reminders

Files returns on time

Helps you avoid penalties

If you’re always rushing last-minute, something’s off.

Helping You Manage Cash Flow

Accounting isn’t just about the past it’s also about the future. You should get:

Cash flow projections

Budget advice

Help planning for big expenses

Cash flow problems don’t just “happen” they build up slowly and can be avoided.

Tracking Your Payroll & Deductions

Even 1 employee means payroll compliance. Your accountant should:

Process salaries

File UIF & PAYE

Keep accurate records for audits

Payroll mistakes cost money and trust — and they’re 100% preventable.

Giving You Strategic Business Advice

Your accountant should feel like a business partner, not just a number-cruncher. They should:

Identify trends in your numbers

Suggest ways to reduce expenses

Alert you to problems early

Great accountants help businesses grow — not just survive.

Helping small businesses in South Africa get control of their books, stay SARS-compliant, and grow with confidence.
